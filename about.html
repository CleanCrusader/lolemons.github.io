Our Story

We picked one fruit and stayed with it.

Most cleaning brands chase every scent and surface at once. We make lemon oil — full stop — and we think that focus shows up in the bottle.

Why lemon, specifically

Walk down any cleaning aisle and "citrus" almost always means orange — it's the cheaper oil to source at scale, so it ended up everywhere. Lemon oil carries a sharper, cleaner top note and the same d-limonene grease-cutting power. We started Lots of Lemon because we wanted that scent, that strength, without diluting it into a fragrance footnote on a long ingredient list.

Cold-pressed, not synthesized

Cold-pressing extracts oil directly from the peel without heat, which keeps the natural compounds — including d-limonene — intact. A lot of "lemon-scented" cleaners use synthetic fragrance instead of real oil, because it's cheaper and more consistent at scale. We went the other way: real peel, real press, and a concentrate format so you're buying oil instead of paying to ship water.

Small batch, for now

We're a small operation selling primarily through Amazon today, with wholesale orders fulfilled directly. As the lineup grows, it'll stay anchored to the same idea: one fruit, done properly, rather than a sprawling line of citrus-adjacent products.
